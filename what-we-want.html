The vision

Restore European Self-Determination

European unification is not an ideological end in itself. It is the indispensable instrument of sovereignty — and sovereignty is the precondition of freedom.

How we think

A non-partisan project.

The constitutional question precedes the political ones.

Generation Europe maintains deliberate non-partisan neutrality. The constitutional question of building European sovereignty precedes the political questions that follow from it. Once Europe possesses real instruments of self-determination, citizens can compete legitimately over how to use them.

The priority hierarchy is therefore explicit:

  1. First: the infrastructure of European sovereignty must be built.
  2. Then: democratic competition can take place within sovereign institutional frameworks worth competing over.
The architecture

Seven pillars of sovereignty.

These are the domains where Europe must speak with one voice. Anything less is not sovereignty — it is a more elaborate form of subordination.

PILLAR 01

Defence and Security

Unified armed forces with integrated strategic command. A sovereign nuclear deterrent and credible deterrence posture. The capacity to defend the continent without external dependency.

PILLAR 02

Foreign Policy

A single European voice in international affairs through coherent diplomacy and unified negotiating power. The end of fragmented bilateral diplomacy that allows external actors to divide and rule.

PILLAR 03

Energy and Critical Resources

A continental energy architecture ensuring supply security and strategic autonomy through diversified sourcing. Energy is the substrate of every other capacity.

PILLAR 04

Unified Market and Capital Base

A fully integrated economic space with free circulation of goods, services, labour, and capital across the continent. The deepest single market in the world, in fact and not only in name.

PILLAR 05

Digital Sovereignty

European stewardship of critical digital systems, cloud infrastructure, and data governance under European jurisdiction. The digital layer is no longer optional infrastructure.

PILLAR 06

Continental Infrastructure

Strategic coordination of ports, rail, energy transmission, space assets, and supply chains as interconnected systems serving the continent as a whole.

PILLAR 07

Strategic Industrial Policy

Institutional frameworks for sovereignty-critical sectors — defence, microelectronics, AI, biotechnology — that recognise these are political, not merely economic, capacities.

The architecture of unification

Federal, by design.

Centralised for unity. Decentralised for pluralism. Both, on purpose.

The institutional vision.

A federal European state structure: centralised for unity, decentralised for pluralism. Combining democratic legitimacy with executive effectiveness, and direct democratic participation through digital platforms.

The constitutional puzzle.

The Franco-German asymmetry is the central problem. France controls nuclear deterrence. Germany controls industrial capacity. Both are essential. Structural safeguards against internal hegemony are therefore prerequisite, not afterthought, to any unification project.

Where to begin.

The current European Union provides the institutional compatibility foundation. But unification need not wait for unanimity. A core group of willing states can initiate the project, with others joining as the political and economic gravity becomes irresistible.

How we get there

Inside-out.

Sovereignty is built before it is asserted.

The Inside-Out strategy.

Build internal strength first — economic, military, technological integration. Systematically reduce dependencies. Then transform external alliances from necessity into equal partnership. Sovereignty is built before it is asserted.

On unity and diversity.

Unity does not mean uniformity. Switzerland and Canada show that successful federal systems protect cultural and linguistic diversity rather than threatening it. A united Europe will be more European — not less — because it will be European in fact, not only in sentiment.
