The vision

European Sovereignty

European unification is not an ideological end in itself. It is the indispensable instrument of sovereignty, and sovereignty is the precondition of freedom.

The premise

Continental problems require continental authority.

Twenty-seven nation-states cannot defend what they cannot coordinate.

The domains that determine Europe's position in the world (defence, foreign policy, energy, capital markets, technology and industrial capacity) all require continental scale. Fragmentation in these areas is not inefficiency. It is incapacity.

Freedom without power becomes dependence. Prosperity without sovereignty becomes vulnerability.

European unification is therefore not an abstract ideal. It is the only answer adequate to the scale of the problem, and the seven pillars below are the domains where European sovereignty must be built.

The architecture

Seven pillars of sovereignty.

These are the domains where Europe must speak with one voice. Anything less is not sovereignty, it is a more elaborate form of subordination.

PILLAR 01

Defence and Security

Unified armed forces with integrated strategic command. A sovereign nuclear deterrent and credible deterrence posture. The capacity to defend the continent without external dependency.

PILLAR 02

Foreign Policy

A single European voice in international affairs through coherent diplomacy and unified negotiating power. The end of fragmented bilateral diplomacy that allows external actors to divide and rule.

PILLAR 03

Energy and Critical Resources

A continental energy architecture ensuring supply security and strategic autonomy through diversified sourcing. Energy is the substrate of every other capacity.

PILLAR 04

Unified Market

A fully integrated economic space with free circulation of goods, services, labour and capital across the continent. A market that operates at continental scale in practice, not only in treaty.

PILLAR 05

Digital Sovereignty

The digital layer is no longer optional infrastructure: it is the nervous system of every modern economy and state. European stewardship of critical digital systems, cloud infrastructure and data governance under European jurisdiction is a condition of sovereignty, not a preference.

PILLAR 06

Continental Infrastructure

Strategic coordination of ports, rail, energy transmission, space assets and supply chains — because infrastructure is the physical expression of sovereignty. A continent whose logistics are fragmented cannot act as one.

PILLAR 07

Strategic Industrial Policy

Sovereignty-critical sectors (defence, microelectronics, AI, biotechnology) cannot be left to market forces alone. These are not merely economic capacities. They are the foundations of political autonomy. A Europe unable to produce its own semiconductors or manufacture its own defence systems cannot defend its own choices. Industrial policy, here, is security policy.

The structure

Federal where it must be. Local where it can be.

Unified on sovereignty. Decentralised on everything else.

The optimal structure is federal: strong central authority on the domains that determine our position in the world, full local autonomy on everything else.

Unified, by necessity. The seven pillars above operate at continental scale by definition. Defence cannot be split into twenty-seven commands. A single market cannot have twenty-seven trade policies. Digital sovereignty cannot exist in fragments. These domains require unified European authority because partial sovereignty, in these areas, is no sovereignty at all.

Decentralised, by design. Everything that does not require continental coordination remains where it belongs: with regions, cities, communities and individuals. Language, culture, education, civic traditions, local administration and civil law are not obstacles to European unity. Europe's diversity is its strength, not a problem to be solved.

The principle is subsidiarity: match the level of governance to the scale of the problem. Power flows up only when continental scale is required, and stays close to people by default. A sovereign Europe is not a uniform Europe; it is the only structure that protects European diversity by giving it real power to defend itself.
