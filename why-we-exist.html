Why We Exist

To contribute to the recovery of our freedom — defined not as a catalogue of individual rights, but as a people's capacity to determine its own collective destiny.

Defining freedom

Freedom is collective.

Procedural rights matter only because a sovereign community guarantees them.

Freedom in the conventional liberal sense — procedural rights, individual liberties — assumes the existence of a sovereign political community capable of guaranteeing those rights. We take that community for granted. We should not.

Freedom, in the deeper sense, is the capacity of a people to shape its own destiny and its place in the world as an autonomous actor. Without that collective autonomy, individual rights become privileges granted by powers we do not control.

For seventy years, Europe existed as a geopolitical province: prosperous, stable, and fundamentally subordinate.
The cost

Seventy years as a province.

Pax Americana ended. We are now in a historical limbo.

The Pax Americana was a generous arrangement. It allowed Europe to rebuild, prosper, and develop the most ambitious social model in human history. But it was always conditional. It required us to outsource defence, technology platforms, monetary architecture, and ultimately strategic direction.

That arrangement is ending. Not because Europeans chose differently — but because the United States is rationally choosing to disengage. We are now living in a historical limbo: no longer a protected province, not yet a sovereign actor.

What comes next

Generational work.

Recovering freedom is the continental project of our lifetime.

Recovering freedom is not a matter of policy adjustment. It requires the construction of a continental sovereign capacity — political, economic, military, technological. This is not a task of months or years. It is generational work.

It will not happen through appeals to good will. It requires organised, sustained, collective effort — and a movement willing to do the work.

That movement is what we are building.
