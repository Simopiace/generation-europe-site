The movement

Who We Are

Generation Europe is a movement of young Europeans committed to building the political community that the times demand.

The case

The challenge before us.

Continental problems require continental institutions. The math is not subtle.

The challenge.

A continent fragmented into 27 nation-states cannot adequately address the challenges of our era — defence, economic competition, technological autonomy, climate adaptation. These problems operate at continental scale. Our institutions do not.

Our mission.

To build the largest, most organised community of Europeans demanding transformative change: unified armed forces, coordinated foreign policy, integrated economic systems, and democratic institutions giving European citizens direct decision-making authority over the matters that shape their lives.

How we work

Three mechanisms, compounding.

None alone is sufficient. All three together are how political subjects are built.

01

Sustained political education

Building European political consciousness as the precondition for any transformation. The case must be made — clearly, repeatedly, in every member language.

02

Transnational civic mobilization

Coordinating action across borders, languages, and political traditions. A European political subject can only emerge through European political practice.

03

Organised democratic structures

Building the institutional capacity to exert real political pressure — not as advocacy, but as a movement with measurable popular legitimacy.

Who joins us

A coalition, not a faction.

Non-partisan by design. The constitutional question precedes the political ones.

Strategists, lawyers, communicators, organisers, technologists, scholars. Citizens. Students. Professionals. We are non-partisan by design — neither left, nor centre, nor right.

The constitutional question of European sovereignty precedes the political questions that follow from it. First we build the instruments of self-determination. Then we compete legitimately over how to use them.

If you can write, organise, design, code, teach, translate, research, or simply show up — there is a place for you in this work.
