Open letter to Europeans

The historic responsibility of our generation.

— Midnight Driver

The political unification of Europe is the defining challenge of our generation. It is the great test that will determine whether we are remembered as those who secured Europe's future, or as those who watched its slow decline without finding the strength to alter its course. The stakes could not be higher.

Fragmentation among European states is no longer merely an inefficiency: it is a structural vulnerability. As American protection thins and global competition deepens, the old fault lines — economic divergence, energy dependence, frontier insecurity — return to the surface, and European nations begin once again to regard one another with strategic suspicion. Every external power has every incentive to widen those fissures: a Europe that quarrels with itself is a Europe that cannot resist anyone. Twenty-seven foreign policies, twenty-seven industrial strategies, twenty-seven security postures cannot defend what they cannot coordinate. The outcome of inaction is foreordained: dependence, coercion, marginality.

And yet, for the first time in history, there exists a generation of Europeans — Generation Europe — for whom continental identity is not an ideology but a daily reality: friendships woven across national borders, lives studied and worked across the continent, the European condition internalized as natural and authentic. The convergence of strategic necessity and social fact is a window that may never open again.

Generation Europe was founded to seize this moment: to transform a fleeting conjuncture into a durable civic and political force, building the largest pan-European community of Europeans — capable of educating, mobilizing, and rendering inescapable the unification of Europe into a single sovereign state.

Join us. Not because it is easy. Not because it is certain. But because it is necessary — and because we are the only generation that can accomplish it.

"Nothing is more powerful than an idea whose time has come." — Victor Hugo
Why now

For seventy years, Europe was a province.

Prosperous, stable, subordinate. That era is closing — and we are unprepared.

The Pax Americana protected us — and made it unnecessary to develop our own sovereignty. Today Europe stands in a historical limbo: no longer a protected province, not yet a sovereign actor.

The cost of remaining fragmented is no longer theoretical. It is the price we pay every day in defence, technology, energy, and the global order we no longer shape.

Generation Europe exists to recover our freedom — defined as a people's capacity to determine its own collective destiny.

What we want

The seven pillars of European sovereignty.

Sovereignty is not an ideological end. It is the indispensable instrument of freedom. These are the seven domains where Europe must speak with one voice.

01

Defence and Security

Unified armed forces, integrated strategic command, sovereign nuclear deterrent.

02

Foreign Policy

A single European voice in international affairs, coherent diplomacy, unified negotiating power.

03

Energy & Resources

Continental energy architecture, supply security, strategic autonomy through diversified sourcing.

04

Unified Market

A fully integrated economic space with free circulation of goods, services, labour, and capital.

05

Digital Sovereignty

European stewardship of critical digital systems, cloud infrastructure, and data governance.

06

Continental Infrastructure

Strategic coordination of ports, rail, energy transmission, space assets, and supply chains.

07

Strategic Industrial Policy

Institutional frameworks for sovereignty-critical sectors: defence, microelectronics, AI, biotech.

Our plan

Community first. Institutions follow.

Mobilised popular demand is what transforms how institutions respond.

The constitutional bootstrap problem is real: no existing institution has the authority to dissolve nation-states and create new sovereignty. Unanimity rules create gridlock. But legitimacy ultimately rests on popular will — institutions are the forms through which sovereignty is expressed, not its substance.

So we build the substance first.

  1. Digital First. A continental digital presence for political education, coordination, and engagement.
  2. On the Ground. Local chapters in universities and civic organisations turning online participation into action.
  3. Expertise & Alliances. Constitutional scholars, policy specialists, and aligned organisations working in parallel.

The generation

The first European generation.

Most educated, most precarious. The paradox is the fuel.

The most educated and most precarious generation in European history. The first generation that grew up European before being French, Italian, Polish, German.

Erasmus. Free movement. A digital culture without internal borders. A continent that, in our lifetime, became a single cultural space — even as its political institutions remained trapped in the twentieth century.

This is the generation that will build a united Europe.
