Open letter to Europeans

The historic responsibility of our generation.

By Midnight Driver

The political unification of Europe is the defining challenge of our generation. It is the great test that will determine whether we are remembered as those who secured Europe's future, or as those who watched its slow decline without finding the strength to alter its course. The stakes could not be higher.

Fragmentation among European states is no longer merely an inefficiency: it is a structural vulnerability. As American protection thins and global competition deepens, the old fault lines (economic divergence, energy dependence, frontier insecurity) return to the surface, and European nations begin once again to regard one another with strategic suspicion. Every external power has every incentive to widen those fissures: a Europe that quarrels with itself is a Europe that cannot resist anyone. Twenty-seven foreign policies, twenty-seven industrial strategies, twenty-seven security postures cannot defend what they cannot coordinate. The outcome of inaction is clear: dependence, coercion, marginality.

And yet, for the first time in history, there exists a generation of Europeans ("Generation Europe") for whom continental identity is not an ideology but a daily reality: friendships woven across national borders, lives studied and worked across the continent, the European condition internalised as natural and authentic. The convergence of strategic necessity and social fact is a window that may never open again.

Generation Europe was founded to seize this moment: to transform a fleeting conjuncture into a durable civic, cultural and political force, building the largest pan-European community of Europeans capable of educating, mobilising, and rendering inescapable the unification of Europe into a single sovereign state.

Join us. Not because it is easy. Not because it is certain. But because it is necessary, and because we are the only generation that can do it.

"Nothing is more powerful than an idea whose time has come." — Victor Hugo
Europe today

We can no longer escape power.

Multipolarity is back. Force is the currency. Europe is unprepared.

Europe was built around the Pax Americana — security underwritten, strategy outsourced, politics reduced to administration. That arrangement is ending.

As the protection thins and global competition deepens, Europe faces a dual strategic exposure: internally, the return of intra-European tensions; externally, the risk of becoming what every great power has incentive to make it: terrain on which others manoeuvre.
